# #ai-spec-review Spec auto-review

- After writing or substantially revising a spec (#ai-plan) under `docs/working-specs/`, offer an adversarial auto-review and wait for the user's choice; never start one unprompted.
- Spec review is the third application of the role-based review engine (#ai-review-engine), at spec altitude: a **generalist** (`spec-specialist`) and the author alternate in rounds, and the generalist routes to + converges a curated fan-out of domain specialists.
- On opt-in each round: the author (driver) consults the curated specialists the generalist routed to (`roles.yaml` `spec_review: true`); each specialist applies its domain lens to the spec and **reconciles** its prior findings (it reads the current spec, its prior findings, and the diff since its last consult, so it does not re-inject fresh blockers); the generalist then **converges** the specialists' findings with its own cross-cutting critique into one round review (each finding **blocking**/nit, with a stable id and an `origin`), and the author revises the spec and writes a rebuttal (each finding `resolved` or `wontfix`).
- **Authority is split:** the generalist owns the **tag** (and may down-tag a specialist's blocker to nit with a reason, removing it from the open-blocking count); the author owns the **status** (`resolved`/`wontfix`). A specialist's blocker carries its own id and leaves the open set only by a generalist down-tag or an author status -- never silently dropped (the `tagHistory` is the audit trail).
- Where the tool supports sub-agents, the generalist and specialists are separate agents so the critique is independent (the driver dispatches both -- a sub-agent cannot spawn sub-agents, so the generalist routes and the driver executes one round later); otherwise one agent role-plays the generalist and each consulted lens, or a human reviews.
  Per #ai-conversational, each sub-agent dispatch **states an explicit model id** -- specialists the cheapest tier that meets the bar, the generalist a model capable of sustained critical reasoning; the convergence-guard math runs in `guard.mjs`, not the model.
- A **review cycle** is a continuous run of rounds on one spec; round numbering and the convergence guard's state (round count and best open-blocking count) are **per cycle, not global**.
  Substantially revising a spec after a prior cycle converged, stalled, or hit the cap starts a **new cycle** with the count and best reset, even when round numbering continues for the reader.
- Convergence guard, checked after each review in this order: zero open blocking = converged; otherwise two consecutive reviews **within the cycle** that fail to beat the best (lowest) open-blocking count = stalled (earliest the cycle's third review); otherwise a **5-round-per-cycle** cap.
- On stall or cap, stop and ask the user how to proceed, summarizing open blockers and any contested `wontfix`.
- Only the final spec is committed; per-round reviews and rebuttals are ephemeral under `.agentsmith/tmp/spec-review/<spec-dir-name>/` and never committed.
